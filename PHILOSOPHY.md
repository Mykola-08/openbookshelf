# OpenBookshelf Design Philosophy

OpenBookshelf is built on a set of core principles that prioritize the reader's focus, privacy, and personal workflow. Inspired by the "Digital Garden" concept and minimalist productivity tools, our goal is to provide a powerful yet invisible environment for deep reading.

## Core Principles

### 1. Minimalism by Default
Interfaces should start small. Use only what you need. Complexity is a choice, not a mandate. We avoid cluttered sidebars and unnecessary notifications.

### 2. Hyper-Personalization
Every reader is different. From font pairings to database schema, the system adapts to your unique workflow. You shouldn't have to fight the tool to read your way.

### 3. Privacy and Ownership
Your data belongs to you. We favor local storage, exportable formats, and self-hosted options whenever possible. Your reading habits are your own business.

### 4. Performance over Polish
A fast, slightly rugged interface is better than a slow, beautiful one. We aim for both, but speed and responsiveness come first.

### 5. Modular Complexity
Advanced features are hidden behind modules. If you don't use community features or AI, they shouldn't cost you mental overhead or interface space.

### 6. Digital Garden, Not a Warehouse
Your library should feel alive—cultivated over time, reflecting your growth as a reader and thinker. It's a place to think, not just to store files.

---

*"A library is not a collection of books, but a collection of thoughts. The software should never stand between a reader and those thoughts."*
